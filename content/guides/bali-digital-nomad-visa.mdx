# The Ultimate Guide to Bali's Digital Nomad Visa

Bali has long been a haven for digital nomads, but navigating the visa system can be tricky. In this guide, we'll break down the best options for sales professionals looking to relocate.

## The B211A Visit Visa

This is the most popular option for remote workers.

*   **Validity:** 60 days, renewable twice for 60 days each (total 180 days).
*   **Cost:** Approx $300 USD initially, plus renewal fees.
*   **Requirements:** Passport valid for 6 months, proof of funds, and a sponsor letter (usually provided by an agency).

## The Second Home Visa

For those looking for a longer-term stay.

*   **Validity:** 5 or 10 years.
*   **Requirements:** Proof of funds of at least 2 billion IDR (approx $130k USD) in an Indonesian bank account.

## Working Legally

Technically, you cannot work for an Indonesian company on a tourist or visit visa. However, working remotely for a foreign company is generally tolerated as long as you are not earning income within Indonesia.

If you land a job with a local company through **Dream Sales Jobs**, your employer will typically sponsor a **KITAS (Work Permit)**, which gives you full legal residency and working rights.

Don't let visa anxiety stop you. The process is straightforward with the right agency. Focus on landing that dream sales role, and the rest will follow.
